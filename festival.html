FESIVALS IN INDIA

India celebrates a rich tapestry of religious and national festivals, including the "Festival of Lights" Diwali, the "Festival of Colors" Holi, and the Islamic festivals of Eid. Other major celebrations include Ganesh Chaturthi, Durga Puja, Onam, Pongal, and the national holidays of Republic Day and Independence Day. These festivals are diverse, reflecting the country's multicultural and multi-religious society, highlighting themes of triumph, harvest, and national pride.

Diwali

Diwali notes can refer to messages and greetings to share with loved ones, highlighting themes of light, prosperity, and good over evil. They can also refer to educational notes or essays explaining the significance of the festival of lights, which involves lighting diyas, family gatherings, worshipping goddesses like Lakshmi, and celebrating the return of Lord Rama

Holi

Holi is the vibrant Hindu festival celebrating the arrival of spring, the victory of good over evil, and the bonds of love and brotherhood. Celebrated in March, it involves playing with dry colors (gulal) and water, with celebrations including Holika Dahan (a bonfire) the night before. The festival is marked by joyful gatherings, singing, dancing, feasting on traditional sweets like gujiya, and enjoying the refreshing drink thandai

Onam

Onam is an annual harvest festival and cultural celebration in Kerala, India, celebrated in August-September to mark the homecoming of King Mahabali and thank the gods for the year's harvest. Key traditions include creating elaborate Pookalams (flower carpets), enjoying a grand vegetarian feast known as Onasadya, watching Vallam Kali (boat races), and participating in various traditional games and dances like Pulikali (tiger dance) and Thiruvathira Kali
